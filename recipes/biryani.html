Biryani

This aromatic and flavorful chicken biryani is made by layering tender marinated chicken with fragrant basmati rice,
slow-cooked with a blend of spices like cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. Each bite is packed with complex flavors,
enhanced by saffron-infused rice and crispy fried onions. Garnished with fresh coriander and mint,
this biryani is a perfect one-pot meal that's rich in both taste and tradition.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken thighs or drumsticks (bone-in, skinless)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt, to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water (for boiling rice)
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt, to taste

For the Biryani:

  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons ghee (or oil)
  • 2-3 green chilies (optional, for spice)
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
  • Fried onions (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder,
      garam masala, cumin, coriander, lemon juice, and salt. Add the chicken pieces,
      ensuring they're evenly coated.
    • Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight for more flavor).
  2. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse the basmati rice in cold water several times until the water runs clear.
    • Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a large pot. Add salt, cardamom, cloves, bay leaf, and cinnamon stick.
    • Add the rinsed rice and cook until the rice is about 70% cooked (the grains should still be firm). Drain and set aside.
  3. Toast the buns:
    • Heat ghee (or oil) in a large skillet over medium heat.
      Add the thinly sliced onions and fry until golden brown and crispy. Set some aside for garnish.
  4. Cook the Chicken:
    • In the same skillet (add more ghee if needed), add the marinated chicken and cook on medium-high heat
      until the chicken is browned and partially cooked through (about 8-10 minutes).
    • Add the green chilies (if using) and half of the chopped coriander and mint leaves. Stir well and set aside.
  5. Layer the Biryani:
    • In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, start layering the biryani.
    • First, spread a layer of the partially cooked chicken at the bottom.
    • Then, add a layer of the partially cooked rice on top of the chicken.
    • Sprinkle the remaining chopped coriander and mint over the rice.
    • Drizzle the saffron-infused milk on top of the rice.
  6. Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the edges with dough (optional) to ensure no steam escapes.
    • Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes. This process, called "dum," allows the flavors to meld together while the rice finishes cooking.
  7. Serve:
    • Once cooked, gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers without breaking the rice.
    • Garnish with crispy fried onions, extra coriander leaves, and serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or a simple cucumber salad.
